This is a sketch for Genuary 2022, which provides a creative coding prompt for each day of January. This is day 7: "Sol LeWitt Wall Drawing." This is a digital interpretation of Sol LeWitt's Wall Drawing 821a:
A white square divided horizontally and vertically into four equal parts, each with a different direction of alternating flat and glossy bands.
The line pattern is generated and then used as a texture map controlling the shininess coefficient of a plane's material. I've also added a bump map to perturb the normals and make it look a little more like a gallery wall.
